Measures of Central Tendency

This page covers the most common measures: arithmetic mean, median, mode, geometric mean, harmonic mean, weighted mean, midrange, trimmed mean, and winsorized mean. Use the interactive widget at the bottom to try your own data.

When to use each measure

  • Arithmetic mean: good for symmetric distributions without strong outliers.
  • Median: robust to outliers and skewed distributions.
  • Mode: best for categorical data or to find the most frequent value.
  • Geometric mean: for multiplicative processes (growth rates); requires positive values.
  • Harmonic mean: for averages of rates (e.g., average speed over equal distances); sensitive to small values.
  • Trimmed / Winsorized: robust alternatives that reduce the influence of outliers.

Examples

  1. Symmetric: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] — mean = 3, median = 3.
  2. Skewed with outlier: [1, 2, 2, 3, 100] — mean ≈ 21.6 (inflated by the outlier), median = 2 (more representative).
  3. Multiplicative: [1.1, 1.2, 0.9] — geometric mean better represents typical multiplicative change.

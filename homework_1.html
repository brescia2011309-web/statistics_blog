WHAT IS STATISTICS, AND WHY CAN IT BE USEFUL FOR CYBERSECURITY?

Autore: CARLA ASSUNTA BRESCIA / MATRICOLA: 2011309

Statistics is a branch of mathematics, the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. In cybersecurity, it's especially useful to analyze large volumes of cyber data, identify patterns, predict threats, detect anomalies, and measure the effectiveness of security measures to proactively protect systems and data from attacks. In the field of cybersecurity, statistics plays a fundamental role because it allows large amounts of raw data from networks, systems, and users to be transformed into useful knowledge for identifying and preventing cyber threats. Through statistical analysis, it is possible to recognize normal behavior patterns and detect anomalies, such as unusual network traffic or an abnormal number of login attempts, which may indicate an ongoing attack. Statistics also makes it possible to estimate the likelihood of certain events, contributing to risk assessment and the planning of appropriate security measures. Furthermore, artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques, which are increasingly used in cybersecurity, are based on statistical principles that enable them to learn from past data and improve their ability to detect new threats. In summary, statistics provides the essential tools to understand and effectively manage the complexity of digital data, making it an indispensable resource for protecting information and computer systems.
